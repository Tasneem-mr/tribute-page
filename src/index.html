Dr.Abdul Rahman Al-Sumait

"when your aspirations are very high, your body will tire in its pursuit"

Here's a brief about the life of Dr.Abdul Rahman Al-Sumait:

  • 1947 Born in Kuwait.
  • 1952 As a child, he had a room on the roof of the house that he made a medical clinic to play the role of a doctor on his brothers.
  • 1957 At this age, Al-Sumait joined the Scouts and learned various skills such as endurance, patience, cooperation with colleagues and helping others. So he came out with an initiative personality who endures difficulties and is able to solve what he faces with patience and fortitude.
  • 1963 He joined the College of Medicine in Baghdad and later graduated with a Bachelor of Medicine and Surgery.
  • 1974-1978 Then he left for the University of Liverpool in the United Kingdom to obtain a diploma in tropical diseases. Then he traveled to Canada to specialize in the field of digestive system and internal diseases, then worked as a specialist at McGill University - Montreal General Hospital - in internal diseases and then in gastroenterology as a practicing physician
  • 1979-1980 Worked as a Specialist Physician at Queen's College Hospital in London
  • 1980-1983 He worked as a gastroenterologist at Al-Sabah Hospital, Kuwait
  • 1984 Somalia, Kenya, the Kingdom of Mumias and Madagascar, he traveled there with a small amount of money, but with a great wealth consisting of all his family members who were brought up to do good, love benevolence and help people. He founded the "Al-Awn" association and rolled up his sleeves from one African village to another and from one city to another...a love train that did not stop at any station. Schools, establishments, stores, employment centers, digging wells, training teachers and scientists, and fighting hunger not by giving money to the poor, but by training them for a profession and opening institutions to work in them..
  • 2013 Abdul Rahman Al-Sumait died after a long suffering with illness and a busy career in the field of Islamic da’wah and charitable works.

“I stayed in Africa for 11 years, and I did not visit any country or tribe without finding them mentioning Al-Sumait with goodness.”

Wadah Khanfar, former director of Al-Jazeera
